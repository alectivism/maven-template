# Research Skill

## Process

### Step 1: Define Scope
- What question are we trying to answer?
- Who is the audience for this research? (internal, board, members, Greg)
- What depth is needed? (quick scan vs. deep dive)
- Any specific sources to prioritize or avoid?

### Step 2: Search Strategy
Run parallel searches across multiple angles:
- Industry publications (AdAge, AdWeek, Marketing Week, Digiday, eMarketer)
- Academic/research sources if relevant
- Competitor/peer organization activities
- Recent news and developments
- MMA's own published research for comparison

### Step 3: Synthesize Findings

Structure output as:

## Research: [Topic]
**Date:** [today's date]
**Scope:** [what was researched and why]

### Key Findings
1. [Finding] — [source, date]
2. [Finding] — [source, date]
3. [Finding] — [source, date]

### Analysis
[2-3 paragraphs connecting findings to MMA's context]
[How does this relate to MMA's strategic priorities?]
[What are the implications for MMA members?]

### Recommendations
- [Action item based on findings]
- [Opportunity or risk to flag]

### Sources
- [URL — title — date accessed]

## MMA Lens
Always frame findings through MMA's perspective:
- How does this impact MMA's 825+ members?
- Does this relate to any of the four think tanks (MATT, MOSTT, ALTT, DATT)?
- Is there a Future Lab (CAP, A3, ACE, SIFT, AURA) connection?
- Does MMA have existing research that supports or contradicts this?
- Is this relevant to any 2026 strategic priorities?
